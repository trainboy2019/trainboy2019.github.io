Paradoxes

Presentation by Ike Rolader

A Short Summary

Paradoxes are arguments that seem sound, but are self contradictory.

  • Some reference themselves to reach a conclusion.
  • Some use recursion or infinity.
  • Some rely on vagueness of words.

What are some examples?

Famous Paradoxes

  • The Sorites Paradox
  • The Omnioptence Paradox
  • The Liar Paradox

The Sorites Paradox

  • Imagine you have a heap of sand.
  • If you take away one grain, is it still a heap?
  • If so, at what point is it no longer a heap?

The Omnipotence Paradox

  • This paradox is pretty straightforward.
  • Could God create a boulder so heavy that he could not lift it?

The Liar Paradox

  • This may be the simplest of the paradoxes so far.
  • This statement is false.
  • Is that statement true or false?

Solving Paradoxes

The Sorites Paradox

  • This is considered a paradox because it uses the vaugeness of the word heap.
  • Some people try to say that there is a cut-off of the size of a heap, but we don't know it yet.
  • Some people said 4 as that's the smallest pyramid you could make.
  • Some people say it's unsolvable, and say that the answers are just avoiding the heart of the paradox.

The Omniopotence Paradox

  • This is a paradox because creates a plausible task, but that task then creates a contradiction with the concept of omnipotence.
  • Some solutions have been proposed, but they just complicate the paradox even more.

The Liar Paradox

  • This is a paradox because it refrences itself to reach a conclusion.
  • Many people have tried to solve it, but no one seems to be able to do it completely.
  • This may just be an unsolvable paradox.

Conclusion

  • Throughout history, new paradoxes have been created.
  • Old ones have been solved.
  • Some haven't been solved yet.
  • Others may not be solvable.
