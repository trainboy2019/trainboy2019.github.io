The History of Gold

Presentation by Ike Rolader

A Short Summary

Gold has been a critical element in human history. It has...

  • Been used as a form of currency for thousands of years.
  • Been used to signify royalty or great status.
  • Been critical in technology today!

What else can we do with it?

The Properties of Gold

  • Gold is the most malleable of the metals.
  • Gold can also reflect infrared light, making it great at conducting heat.
  • It is also good at conducting electricity.
  • But what about more uses in chemistry?

The Nobility of Gold

  • Gold is the most noble of the noble metals.
  • What does that mean?
  • It means that gold is resistant to corrosion and oxidation in moist air.
  • But are there any other uses?

Gold and Mercury

  • Gold is not very reactive, but it does dissolve in mercury to form an amalgam at room temperature, and an alloy at higher temperatures.
  • Sure enough, this changes the properties.
  • This can be used to control the melting point of gold.
  • Give me some numbers!

Numbers

  • Standard Atomic Weight: 196.97
  • Element Number: 79
  • Melting Point: 1337.33 K
  • Boiling Point: 3243 K
  • Ok, but what does it look like?

The Appearance of Gold

  • Color: Metallic Yellow
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid
  • Edible: Yes
  • How is it Mined?

The Mining of Gold

  • Gold comes form ores underground.
  • The largest producer is China with 450 tonnes of gold in 2014.
  • Prior to China, South Africa was the largest gold producer.
  • How is it Extracted?

The Extraction of Gold

  • Ore grades can have about 0.5 parts per million of gold, thus making it invisible to the naked eye, as ore grades are about 30PPM.
  • It costs about $317 per troy ounce.
  • How is it Refined?

The Refinement of Gold

  • One way to refine gold is to use the Wohlwill process which is based on electrolysis.
  • This method is much more complex, but results in heigher purity than other methods.
  • Another way to refine gold is to dissolve it in aqua regia.

THe Refinement of Gold Part 2

  • Nitric acid is an oxidizer, which forms gold ions.
  • The Hydroxide acid then reacts with the gold ions to form tetrachloroaurate(III) anions.
  • The gold then dissolves to becom chloroauric acid.
  • Then the excess aqua regia is boiled off.
  • Finally, it is selectively reduced with sulfur dioxide, hydrazine, and other compounds.
  • How is the price controlled?

The Price of Gold

  • In the United States, the free marked changes the price based on supply and demand.
  • However, in 1934, President Roosevelt signed an execuutive order that made it illegal to have gold. After that, gold was set at $35.00 an ounce.
  • In London. the price was set twice a day to match the US market.
